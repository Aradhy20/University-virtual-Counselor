TMU Hostel & Accommodation – FAQ 

General Hostel Information

Q: How many hostels does TMU have?

A: TMU has five hostels on campus — two for boys and three for girls — with a total capacity to accommodate 4600 students.

Q: What kind of rooms are available in TMU hostels?

A: Rooms are available in 2-seater, 3-seater, and 4-seater options. Each type comes with either attached or common bathroom options.

Q: What furnishings are provided in each hostel room?

A: Each room includes a bed, wardrobe, study table, chair, and a desert cooler. Rooms with air conditioning are also available for additional charges.

Q: Is Wi-Fi provided in hostels?

Q: Is Wi-Fi provided in hostels?

A: Yes, high-speed Wi-Fi is available across all hostels.

Q: Are the hostels safe?

A: Yes, hostels are under 24x7 security and surveillance.

Food & Dining

Q: What kind of food is served in the hostel mess?

A: Only pure vegetarian food is served. Non-vegetarian food is strictly prohibited on campus.

Q: Is food served in a mess or delivered to rooms?

A: Food is served in spacious dining halls within the hostels. Students are not allowed to take food or utensils outside the mess unless permitted by wardens.

Q: Are students responsible for clearing their utensils?

A: Yes, students must clean up after themselves by placing used utensils in the appropriate bins and disposing waste in designated trash drums.

Hostel Fees (2025–26 Academic Year)

Q: What is the hostel fee for 2-seater rooms with attached bathroom and cooler?

A: For Non-Jain students: ₹130,000 per year (₹102,000 lodging + ₹28,000 mess). For Jain students: ₹114,700 per year (₹86,700 lodging + ₹28,000 mess).

Q: What is the hostel fee for 2-seater rooms with common bathroom and cooler?

A: For Non-Jain students: ₹90,000 per year (₹62,000 lodging + ₹28,000 mess). For Jain students: ₹71,400 per year (₹43,400 lodging + ₹28,000 mess).

Q: What is the hostel fee for 3-seater rooms with attached bathroom and cooler?

A: For Non-Jain students: ₹95,000 per year (₹67,000 lodging + ₹28,000 mess). For Jain students: ₹84,950 per year (₹56,950 lodging + ₹28,000 mess).

Q: What is the hostel fee for 3-seater rooms with common bathroom and cooler?

A: For Non-Jain students: ₹80,000 per year (₹52,000 lodging + ₹28,000 mess). For Jain students: ₹64,400 per year (₹36,400 lodging + ₹28,000 mess).

Q: What is the hostel fee for 4-seater rooms with attached bathroom and cooler?

A: For Non-Jain students: ₹75,000 per year (₹47,000 lodging + ₹28,000 mess). For Jain students: ₹67,950 per year (₹39,950 lodging + ₹28,000 mess).

Q: What is the hostel fee for 4-seater rooms with common bathroom and cooler?

A: For Non-Jain students: ₹60,000 per year (₹32,000 lodging + ₹28,000 mess). For Jain students: ₹50,400 per year (₹22,400 lodging + ₹28,000 mess).

Q: Are there any discounts for Jain students?

A: Yes. Jain students receive a 30% exemption in lodging fees for common bathroom rooms and 15% exemption for attached bathroom rooms.

Facilities and Lifestyle

Q: What additional facilities are available in the hostel?

A: Facilities include a visitor's lounge, common room with TV, indoor games, study areas, water purifiers, water coolers, 24/7 power backup, solar-heated water supply, laundry services, gym, and access to the on-campus super specialty hospital.

Q: Are wellness activities available for students in hostels?

A: Yes. Regular yoga and meditation sessions are organized in the mornings and evenings.

Hostel Administration & Wardens

Q: Who manages the hostels?

A: Each hostel is managed by resident and non-resident wardens. They act as local guardians and help students with outstation permissions, academic and personal support, and hostel coordination.

Hostel Rules & Conduct

Q: Can students cook inside the hostel rooms?

A: No, cooking in hostel rooms is strictly prohibited.

Q: What is the curfew policy for students?

A: Male students must return to campus by 6:00 PM and be inside the hostel by 10:30 PM. Female students must return to campus by 4:00 PM and be in the hostel by 7:00 PM. All students are expected to be in their rooms by 11:00 PM.

Q: What is the rule for going out of station?

Q: What is the rule for going out of station?

A: Students must submit a leave form to the warden, attach a parent's consent letter, collect a gate pass, and register their exit with security. They must carry their ID card while leaving.

Q: Can students change or share rooms on their own?

A: No, room changes or sharing are only allowed with written permission from the warden within the first few days of room allocation.

Q: Is there a rule about damaging property?

A: Yes. Students damaging hostel property will bear the repair cost and may face disciplinary action.

Q: Are guests allowed inside the hostels?

Q: Are guests allowed inside the hostels?

A: Only parents/guardians can visit after permission. Guest accommodation (limited) is available on payment. Only female guests are allowed in girls' hostels.

Q: Can students bring valuables or large cash?

A: Students are advised against keeping large amounts of cash or valuables. They are responsible for their personal belongings.

Q: Is there a fine for extra electricity usage?

A: Yes. 100 units per month are free. Beyond that, students are charged ₹6 per unit.

Q: What are banned items in hostels?

A: Smoking, pan masala, alcohol, drugs, gambling, non-veg food, weapons, explosives, and loud music are strictly banned.

Q: Can wardens enter rooms at any time?

Q: Can wardens enter rooms at any time?

A: Yes. The Chief Warden or Warden can open rooms in case of violations or security concerns.
