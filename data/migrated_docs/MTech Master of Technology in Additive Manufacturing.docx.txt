MTech (Master of Technology) in Additive Manufacturing

Tell me about MTech (Master of Technology) in Additive Manufacturing

MTech (Master of Technology) in Additive Manufacturing is a two-year postgraduate programme, spanning over four semesters, that equips students with advanced skills in 3D printing technologies, materials science, and computer-aided design. The curriculum includes subjects such as Additive Manufacturing Technologies, Materials Science, Computer-Aided Design (CAD), Advanced Manufacturing Processes

, Advanced Manufacturing Processes, and Metal and Polymer Additive Manufacturing.To be eligible, candidates must hold a bachelor’s degree in engineering or a related discipline

. Graduates can pursue careers in industries like aerospace, automotive, healthcare, etc, where additive manufacturing is transforming traditional production methods.

Q: M.Tech Additive Manufacturing programme curriculum

A: The M.Tech in Additive Manufacturing at Teerthanker Mahaveer University is structured to provide a balance of theoretical knowledge and practical experience

. The programme includes core subjects, elective courses, and a research project, ensuring that students gain a holistic understanding of Additive Manufacturing.Core Subjects in M.Tech Additive ManufacturingAdditive Manufacturing Technologies: This subject covers the various Additive Manufacturing technologies, including Stereolithography (SLA), Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)

, Selective Laser Sintering (SLS), and Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM)

. Students learn how to select the appropriate technology for different applications.Materials Science and Engineering: Understanding the materials used in Additive Manufacturing is critical for ensuring the quality and durability of printed parts

. This subject explores the properties of metals, polymers, and composites used in Additive Manufacturing.Computer-Aided Design (CAD): CAD is a fundamental tool in Additive Manufacturing, allowing engineers to design complex parts with precision

. This subject teaches students how to use CAD software to create digital models that can be printed using AM technologies.Advanced Manufacturing Processes: This subject delves into the advanced manufacturing processes that complement Additive Manufacturing, such as hybrid manufacturing and post-processing techniques

, ensuring that students have a well-rounded understanding of the manufacturing lifecycleElectives in M.Tech Additive ManufacturingMetal Additive Manufacturing: This specialisation focuses on the use of metals in AM, which is particularly important in industries like aerospace and automotive

. Students learn about the challenges and solutions in metal AM, including issues related to material properties and process optimisation.Polymer Additive Manufacturing: Polymer AM is widely used for producing lightweight and flexible parts

. This specialisation covers the various types of polymers used in AM, their properties, and their applications in different industries.Bioprinting: Bioprinting is an emerging field within AM that involves printing living tissues and organs

. This specialisation explores the technologies and materials used in bioprinting, as well as the ethical and regulatory considerations.Research Labs and FacilitiesTeerthanker Mahaveer University offers advanced research labs equipped with the latest Additive Manufacturing technologies, allowing students to conduct cutting-edge research.
