Tell me about MSc in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing

MSc in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing is a two-year postgraduate programme divided into four semesters

. The MSc in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing programme will focus on the fields of obstetric/neonatal nursing, management of high-risk pregnancy, and gynaecological nursing

, and other related areas concerned with maternal and child health. Eligibility for the MSc in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing programme requires a BSc Nursing degree with an aggregate score as required and 1-year experience as a Registered Nurse or midwife may also required. MSc in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing programme graduates can pursue their career by working as a Clinical Nurse

their career by working as a Clinical Nurse Specialist

, a Nurse Educator, a Supervisor of Nursing, and a Research Nurse in a wide range of healthcare services.

Q: M.Sc.(Nursing) in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing Programme Curriculum

A: The M.Sc. (N) in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing at TMU is a two-year programme, divided into four semesters. Each semester is meticulously structured to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical experience. The curriculum is designed to cover essential subjects in the first year, laying a strong foundation in advanced nursing practices

. The second year focuses on specialisation in Obstetrics & Gynaecology, allowing students to delve deeper into the complexities of women’s health and maternal care.Year 1: Foundational CoursesIn the first year, students will engage with core subjects such as Advanced Nursing Practice, Research Methodology, and Nursing Education

. These courses provide a solid base in nursing theory and practice, preparing students for the advanced topics they will encounter in their specialisation.Year 2: Advanced Specialisation in Obstetrics & GynaecologyThe second year is dedicated to the specialisation in Obstetrics & Gynaecology Nursing

. Courses in this year include Obstetric Nursing, Neonatal Nursing, Gynaecological Nursing, and High-Risk Obstetrics. These subjects are designed to provide in-depth knowledge and skills required to care for women during pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum
